Research Session

I proposed and self-led my own design and research projects, and worked closely with the development team and CEO to define the user needs, business requirements and problem space for shaping vioce interaction opportunities.

I can’t showcase most work, but check out my process

1. Product Insight

To understand EasySize’s advantages and the system-level design language. I performed product analysis about online shopper behavior as well as online shops’ size guide, and 4 of other sizing competitors.

2. Talk to the end-users

I spoke to several online shoppers with different background and online shopping preferences. These user interviews helped me to understand of why and how do people shop online. It became my foundation to design, based on the narrative of user needs.

3. Design, Prototyping, Testing

In the highly iterative process, I held design critics and research sessions to pull in developers, CEO and of course users to help me understand my design.

Journey Takeaways

I don’t know much about sizing nor online shopping much before I join EasySize. The user interviews have definitely helped me a lot to get insights about our product design and directions. And it also gives huge motivation to learn new design and mockup tools to present my idea in high definition.
